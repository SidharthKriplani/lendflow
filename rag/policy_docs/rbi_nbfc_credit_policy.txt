RBI MASTER DIRECTIONS — NON-BANKING FINANCIAL COMPANIES (NBFC)
CREDIT RISK MANAGEMENT AND LOAN ORIGINATION GUIDELINES
(Synthetic Reference Document — For LendFlow RAG Training Only)

CHAPTER 1: INCOME ASSESSMENT AND FIXED OBLIGATION TO INCOME RATIO (FOIR)

1.1 Definition of FOIR
The Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) is defined as the ratio of total fixed monthly obligations
(including all existing EMIs, proposed loan EMI, rent, and any other contractual fixed payments) to
the net monthly income of the borrower. FOIR is a primary credit underwriting parameter for all
retail loan products including vehicle loans, personal loans, and home loans.

1.2 FOIR Thresholds by Employment Category

1.2.1 Salaried Borrowers
For borrowers with confirmed salaried employment evidenced by salary slips from an employer and
salary credits visible in bank statements, the maximum permissible FOIR shall not exceed 55% of
net monthly income. Applications where FOIR exceeds 55% shall be classified as high-risk and
routed for senior credit officer review or rejection as per individual NBFC policy.

1.2.2 Self-Employed Borrowers
For self-employed individuals including proprietors, partners, and professional practitioners,
income estimation is based on bank statement analysis over a minimum 6-month period. The maximum
permissible FOIR for self-employed borrowers shall not exceed 50% of estimated monthly income.
Given higher income volatility for self-employed applicants, a more conservative FOIR ceiling
is mandated to maintain portfolio quality.

1.2.3 Contract and Gig Workers
Borrowers categorized as contract workers, gig economy participants, or those with irregular
income patterns shall be assessed with FOIR limits not exceeding 45% of average monthly income.
Income averaging shall be computed over a minimum 6-month lookback period. Cash flow volatility
above 30% month-over-month shall be flagged as a risk factor.

1.3 Income Documentation Requirements
Net monthly income must be verifiable through one or more of the following: (a) salary slips for
minimum 3 consecutive months, (b) Form 16 or ITR for the preceding 2 financial years,
(c) bank account statements showing consistent credit entries for minimum 6 months.
Estimated income based on unverified declarations shall not be accepted as sole income proof.
FOIR exceeding limits or income that cannot be verified through documentary evidence shall
trigger an INCOME_VERIFIABLE policy flag requiring escalation.

1.4 EMI Obligation Identification
All existing EMIs visible in bank statements including home loan EMIs, vehicle loan EMIs, personal
loan EMIs, credit card minimum payments, and any other fixed debit obligations must be identified
and included in FOIR calculation. Undisclosed obligations identified through bureau checks shall
be added to FOIR. Applications where EMI obligations are unclear or partially visible shall
attract the INCOME_VERIFIABLE flag.

CHAPTER 2: KYC REQUIREMENTS FOR VEHICLE LOAN ORIGINATION

2.1 Mandatory KYC Documents
All loan applicants must complete KYC as per the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) and
RBI KYC Master Directions. The following documents are mandatory for individual borrowers:

2.1.1 Identity Proof (any one)
Aadhaar Card (12-digit UID issued by UIDAI), Passport, Voter ID, Driving License, or PAN Card.
For vehicle loan applicants, Driving License is the preferred identity document as it additionally
validates driving competency for the financed asset.

2.1.2 Address Proof (any one)
Aadhaar Card (if not used as identity proof), Utility bills not older than 3 months, Bank account
statement not older than 3 months, Registered rent agreement.

2.1.3 PAN Card
PAN is mandatory for all loan applications above Rs. 50,000. PAN must be linked to Aadhaar as per
Income Tax Act provisions. Applications without a valid PAN shall be rejected or flagged for
escalation pending PAN submission. Non-availability of PAN shall trigger the KYC_COMPLETE flag
as an incomplete KYC condition.

2.2 KYC Verification Standards
Name on KYC documents must match across all submitted documents within acceptable variation
(initials, middle name differences permissible). Significant name mismatches indicating different
persons shall trigger a KYC_COMPLETE flag and require physical verification or notarized affidavit.
Date of birth must be consistent across identity documents. Address must be current and verifiable.

2.3 Digital KYC
Video KYC (V-CKY) is permissible as per RBI circular RBI/2019-20/190. Aadhaar-based OTP
authentication through authorized KUA entities is acceptable. Physical document submission
remains mandatory for loan amounts above Rs. 5 lakh.

CHAPTER 3: VEHICLE ASSESSMENT AND VALUATION GUIDELINES

3.1 Vehicle Valuation Standards
For used vehicle loan applications, vehicle valuation must be conducted by an approved valuer
empaneled with the lending NBFC. The assessed value shall be determined by reference to:
(a) age and make/model of the vehicle, (b) odometer reading and service history,
(c) physical condition inspection, (d) prevailing market price for comparable vehicles.

3.2 Vehicle Condition Grading
NBFCs shall adopt a standardized vehicle condition grading system:
Grade A (Excellent): Minor wear, full service history, no structural damage. LTV up to 80%.
Grade B (Good): Moderate wear, mostly complete service history, no major damage. LTV up to 70%.
Grade C (Average): Visible wear, incomplete service history, minor repairs noted. LTV up to 60%.
Grade D (Poor): Significant wear, major repairs required, incomplete documentation. LTV up to 45%.
Applications where vehicle condition is Grade D or the inspection has not been completed shall
be escalated for senior review. Vehicles failing inspection shall be rejected outright.

3.3 RC Encumbrance Check
Registration Certificate (RC) must be verified for encumbrances through the Parivahan Vahaan
portal or equivalent state RTO database. Any active hypothecation, charge, or lien on the
vehicle's RC constitutes RC encumbrance. Applications with RC_ENCUMBRANCE detected shall be
mandatorily rejected. Financing an encumbered vehicle without prior NOC from the existing
financier is prohibited under securitization and lending regulations. RC encumbrance is a
hard-block condition with no exceptions.

3.4 Odometer and Mileage Considerations
Excessive odometer readings relative to vehicle age shall be flagged. The general guideline is
15,000–20,000 km per year as normal usage for private vehicles. Vehicles exceeding 25,000 km
per year of age shall be considered high-usage and attract stricter valuation and LTV limits.

CHAPTER 4: RED FLAGS AND SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION INDICATORS

4.1 Definition of Red Flags in Credit Applications
The following patterns in bank statement analysis shall be treated as red flags requiring
mandatory escalation or rejection:

4.1.1 Round-Trip Transactions
Credits immediately followed (within 2-3 business days) by equivalent debits of the same amount
indicate potential circular fund flow. Round-trip transactions used to artificially inflate
apparent income shall be identified and excluded from income computation. Applications where
more than 20% of total credits are identified as round-trip transactions shall be flagged as
RED_FLAGS and mandatorily rejected.

4.1.2 Large Unexplained Cash Deposits
Cash deposits exceeding Rs. 2 lakh in a single instance or Rs. 5 lakh in a month without
corresponding business explanation constitute a red flag under PMLA obligations. Such deposits
shall not be included in income calculations.

4.1.3 Frequent Overdraft Utilization
Bank accounts consistently operating close to or below zero balance, with frequent overdraft
utilization, indicate poor financial health. Negative closing balance in more than 2 of the
preceding 6 months shall be flagged.

4.1.4 Salary Credit Irregularity
For salaried applicants, salary credits must be consistent in amount (±10% tolerance for
variable pay components) and timing (±5 working days). Irregular or declining salary credits
shall trigger income verification escalation.

4.2 Bureau-Level Red Flags
Delinquency in existing credit obligations (DPD > 30 days in preceding 12 months), write-offs,
settlements below contracted amount, and active NPA status with any lender shall constitute
bureau-level red flags. Credit bureau verification is mandatory for all loan applications.

CHAPTER 5: LOAN ROUTING AND DECISIONING FRAMEWORK

5.1 Three-Tier Decision Framework
All loan applications shall be classified into one of three decision categories:

5.1.1 APPROVE
Applications meeting all of the following criteria shall be approved within delegated authority:
- FOIR within prescribed limits for the employment category
- KYC complete with all mandatory documents verified
- Vehicle condition Grade A or B, no RC encumbrance
- No red flags identified in bank statement analysis
- Income verifiable through documentary evidence
- No bureau delinquencies in preceding 24 months

5.1.2 ESCALATE (Human Review Required)
Applications with one or more of the following shall be escalated for senior credit officer review:
- FOIR between 5-10 percentage points above limit (soft breach)
- KYC partially complete with minor deficiencies pending resolution
- Vehicle condition Grade C or borderline B/C
- Thin-file borrower (limited credit history, first-time borrower)
- Employment type uncertain or irregular income patterns
- Marginal red flags requiring manual assessment
- Extraction confidence below threshold (system uncertainty)

5.1.3 REJECT
Applications with any of the following hard-block conditions shall be mandatorily rejected:
- FOIR exceeding the applicable limit by more than 10 percentage points
- RC encumbrance detected on vehicle
- Red flags confirmed (circular transactions, fraudulent income inflation)
- KYC fraud indicators or document tampering
- Active NPA or recent write-off on bureau
- Vehicle fails physical inspection

5.2 Audit and Documentation Requirements
All credit decisions must be accompanied by a documented audit trail including:
the application data, decision rationale, policy rules triggered, officer identity,
and timestamp. Audit records must be retained for a minimum of 8 years as per
RBI record-keeping requirements. PII data in audit records must be stored with
appropriate encryption and access controls.
