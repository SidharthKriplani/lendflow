LENDFLOW CREDIT OPERATIONS MANUAL
INTERNAL POLICY DOCUMENT — VERSION 2.1
VEHICLE LOAN UNDERWRITING STANDARDS

SECTION 1: FOIR OPERATIONAL THRESHOLDS

1.1 Approved FOIR Limits by Segment
LendFlow operates the following FOIR thresholds for vehicle loan underwriting decisions:

Salaried — Grade A Employer (MNC, PSU, large corporate): FOIR limit 55%
Salaried — Grade B Employer (mid-size company, stable sector): FOIR limit 55%
Salaried — Grade C Employer (small company, startup, informal sector): FOIR limit 50%
Self-Employed Professional (doctor, CA, lawyer with verified income): FOIR limit 50%
Self-Employed Business Owner (ITR-based income): FOIR limit 50%
Contract / Gig Worker (irregular income): FOIR limit 45%
Employment Type Unknown / Unverified: Escalate, do not approve

When FOIR is within 3 percentage points of the applicable limit (soft breach zone),
the application must be escalated for human review. The credit officer may approve
at their discretion after reviewing all other parameters holistically.

1.2 FOIR Calculation Methodology
Monthly Income = Average of last 3 months salary credits (salaried) or
                 Average of last 6 months net credits (self-employed)
Fixed Obligations = All EMIs identified in bank statement + proposed EMI
FOIR = Fixed Obligations / Monthly Income

EMI identification protocol: any recurring debit of the same amount (±2%)
on a consistent date (±3 days) each month for minimum 2 consecutive months
shall be classified as an EMI obligation.

Income averaging exclusions: large one-time credits (>3x average), suspected
round-trip credits, inter-account transfers, and cash deposits without payslip
correlation shall be excluded from income averaging.

SECTION 2: KYC DECISION MATRIX

2.1 KYC Completeness Definition
KYC is considered COMPLETE when all of the following are verified:
- Valid photo identity proof present and legible
- Current address proof present and not older than 3 months
- PAN Card present and matches identity proof name (within tolerance)
- Date of birth consistent across all identity documents
- Aadhaar number present (masked acceptable for processing, full required for disbursal)

KYC is considered INCOMPLETE when any of the following apply:
- PAN Card absent (KYC_COMPLETE flag triggered, mandatory escalation)
- Name mismatch greater than 2 words difference between documents
- Address proof older than 6 months
- Aadhaar number absent with no alternative identity document
- Documents appear altered or tampered with (hard reject)
- Date of birth inconsistency of more than 1 year between documents

2.2 KYC Verification Outcomes
KYC_VERIFIED: All documents present, consistent, and verified → proceed to income check
KYC_PENDING: Minor deficiencies (e.g., slightly old address proof) → escalate with note
KYC_INCOMPLETE: Material deficiencies (no PAN, name mismatch) → escalate, cannot approve
KYC_FRAUD: Document tampering suspected → hard reject, file SAR if required

SECTION 3: VEHICLE ASSESSMENT STANDARDS

3.1 Condition Grade Definitions and LTV Caps
Grade A — Excellent condition:
  Criteria: < 5 years old, odometer < 60,000 km, no accident history,
            full service records, passes all inspection points
  LTV Cap: 80% of assessed value
  Routing: APPROVE subject to financial parameters

Grade B — Good condition:
  Criteria: 5-8 years old OR odometer 60,000–100,000 km,
            minor wear acceptable, service history mostly complete,
            no structural damage
  LTV Cap: 70% of assessed value
  Routing: APPROVE if borderline B consider financial parameters carefully

Grade C — Average condition:
  Criteria: 8-12 years old OR odometer 100,000–150,000 km,
            visible wear, repairs noted, incomplete service history
  LTV Cap: 60% of assessed value
  Routing: ESCALATE — senior credit officer sign-off required

Grade D — Poor condition:
  Criteria: > 12 years old OR odometer > 150,000 km,
            major repairs needed, structural concerns, failed inspection items
  LTV Cap: 45% — only under exceptional circumstances
  Routing: REJECT in most cases; exceptional approval requires VP credit sign-off

Vehicle inspection not completed: ESCALATE immediately. Disbursement cannot
proceed without a valid inspection report from an empaneled valuer.

3.2 RC Encumbrance Policy
RC Encumbrance = Hard Block. No exceptions. No escalation path. Mandatory rejection.
An encumbered RC means the vehicle is already hypothecated to another financier.
LendFlow cannot create a first charge on the asset. The application must be rejected
and the applicant must be informed to obtain NOC from the existing financier before
reapplying. This rule cannot be overridden by any credit officer. System must record
RC_ENCUMBRANCE as a rejection reason in the audit log.

3.3 Odometer Benchmarks
Normal usage: 15,000 km/year
High usage threshold: 25,000 km/year
Any vehicle where (odometer_km / vehicle_age_years) > 25,000 shall be flagged for
additional scrutiny and downgraded one condition level for LTV calculation purposes.

SECTION 4: RED FLAG DEFINITIONS AND ACTIONS

4.1 Hard-Block Red Flags (Mandatory Rejection)
The following red flags are non-negotiable rejection triggers:
- Round-trip transactions confirmed: credits immediately reversed within 48 hours,
  pattern present in more than 2 instances in 3-month period
- Income inflation scheme: salary credits from unknown entities not matching
  declared employer, inconsistent with Form 16 or offer letter
- Circular fund flow: money returned to originating account within 72 hours
- Fraudulent documents: inconsistent metadata, suspected digital manipulation
- Multiple applications with different income declarations

4.2 Soft Red Flags (Escalation Required)
The following patterns require escalation but do not mandate automatic rejection:
- Cash deposit > Rs. 1 lakh in a single month without business explanation
- Overdraft usage in 1 of last 6 months
- Declining income trend (each of last 3 months lower than prior month)
- Large irregular credits without clear source

4.3 Income Exclusion Rules
The following credits MUST be excluded from income calculation:
- Round-trip amounts (credit followed by same-amount debit within 48 hours)
- Cash deposits without documentary income source
- One-time non-recurring credits (sale of asset, inheritance, gift)
- Inter-bank self-transfers
- Loan disbursement credits from other lenders

SECTION 6: AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Mandatory Audit Fields
Every loan decision must capture: application_id, timestamp, doc_type,
all extracted fields, confidence scores for each field, policy flags triggered
(with rule name and detail), routing decision, reason codes, human_review_required
flag, human_override if applicable, policy chunks cited with source reference,
token usage log, pipeline_version, and processing time.

6.2 PII Handling in Audit Logs
Audit logs must NOT contain raw PII. PAN numbers, Aadhaar numbers, mobile numbers,
bank account numbers, and IFSC codes must be stored as placeholder references only.
The actual PII map must be stored separately in an encrypted store with restricted
access. Audit logs retain only the pii_map_id pointer for traceability.

6.3 Human Override Documentation
When a credit officer overrides a system recommendation (approve an escalation,
reject an approval, or change routing), the override must be documented with:
officer_id, override_reason_text, decision_changed_from, decision_changed_to,
and timestamp. Override rates above 15% signal potential system calibration issues
and shall trigger monthly model performance review.
